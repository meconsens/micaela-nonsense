Micaela Elisa Consens

PhD Candidate · Computer Science · University of Toronto

I'm a Computer Science PhD candidate at the University of Toronto (Moses and Wang labs) working at the intersection of machine learning and genomics. My research asks: can we build genomic models that don't just perform well, but actually encode interpretable biological structure?

I develop methods to interpret what genome language models learn during self-supervised pre-training, design biologically-motivated pre-training objectives, and build multimodal frameworks that align genomic sequences to their functional products.

Research Focus

01

Interpreting genome language models

Characterizing what biological structures are encoded in self-supervised representations — from random initialization through pre-training and fine-tuning — using attention and gradient-based methods.

02

Biologically-informed pre-training

Designing pre-training objectives (evolutionary rate prediction, masked evolution modeling) that incorporate biological structure as a training signal, producing more generalizable and interpretable representations.

03

Multimodal genomic alignment

Building contrastive learning frameworks that align genomic sequences with their functional products — coding sequences to proteins, regulatory DNA to expression — to create representations that are interpretable by design.
